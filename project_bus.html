BusyBus

Overview

Everyone has their own schedule, which can be stressful enough. But having to worry about what time the next bus is and will they make it in time is unncessary additional stress.

The goal of BusyBus is designed to provide real-time information for bus arrival times so that riders will know how long until the next bus and which bus will be arriving.

The Challenge

Due to an expansion by the local city transit system, numerous bus routes have been recently added and many of those routes service the same bus stop. Riders would like to know which is the next arriving bus and how much time they have to get to the bus stop. Riders are currently complaining the most about the bus stop at Washington and State, which has seven bus lines serving the stop.

Understanding the Users

A user survey was done to discover:

- If people use public transporation
- How often public transporation is used
- If people use mobile transit apps to get around
- Their habits when using the mobile transit apps

A total of 10 survey participants.


Key Discoveries

60% are public transit users

Of the public transit users, 100% have some type of transit app downloaded

66.7% check the app for public transit schedules

50% found transit apps to be reliable

50% would initially check the transit app when they have already arrived at the transit stop

33.3% would initially check the app when they are leaving their origin point

From the survey, I discovered what aspects were important to users in a transit app and how they use the apps.

Competitive Analysis

To gain a stronger sense of the market with transportation apps, I performed an analysis on various apps in the market, focusing on Moovit and Citymapper.

Strength
  • • The buttons are large enough for readability as well as less room for error
  • • Simple footer navigation bar consisting of the three most common calls to action for the app
Weakness
  • • Providing users with the most efficient and clearest itineraries
  • • User have stated that real-time information is unreliable
Strength
  • • Map of current location appears on home screen
  • • Saves the user's trip history and uses the historical data from those trips to suggest the current best route
Weakness
  • • The home screen is quite busy (ex: many small buttons)
  • • Not all transportation services offer real-time updates

Through analyzing these competitors, I concluded the following would be opportunities to focus on to build a user-friendly app.

Opportunity
  • • Design a simple home screen to minimize room for error and frustration for users who are on the move
  • • Clearly informing users of their transit options
  • • Ensuring users are shown the best routes
  • • Providing accurate real-time information for transit options

User Personas

I created the following user personas based on the responses from the user survey about the user's motivations and frustrations.

  • Derek
  • Technical Recruiter
  • Age: 35 // Gender: Male
  • Location: San Francisco, CA

  • "I wish the public transit apps would provide the fastest and most direct route to places as well as accurate arrival times."

What is important to users?

These high priority tasks were determined through the user survey conducted.

  • "I want to see the closest transit stops to my current location."
  • "I want to see how far it is to my desired transit stop."
  • "I want to see which transit options are the closest to my current location."
  • "I want to see how long until the next bus/train."

User Flow

I determined the minimum viable product features to hone in on from establishing the user stories.

User wants detailed information on a bus line

Product Vision

3 key focus areas:

  • • Next bus arrival times
  • • Map feature
  • • Transit information


First Stop
  • In this frame,

  • - Lists the transit option closest to the user's current location

  • - Map shows both the current location of the user as well as the desired bus stop

  • - Segmented control allows the user to toggle between the inbound and outbound bus times

Visual Design

I chose blue as the main color theme since it is associated with responsibility and reliability. These themes are important for a transit app since for some users, public transit is a key factor in their daily lives and they depend on its efficiency.
For the content in the frame, I decided to include the time of arrival for the next three buses because not all users will be at the bus stop, which I learned from the responses of my user survey. Therefore, with future bus arrival times, users are able to plan ahead if they wish.

Next Stop

Changes made for this iteration were:

  • - Refresh arrows were changed to Share Location function: It was redundant since the frame can be refreshed by swiping down
  • - Bookmark icon was replaced with a Star Icon to represent the user's favorites
  • - Extended the top navigation bar to include the back button and the other icons to give a sense of separation from the key content on the screen
  • - Added space between the icons as they were too close together
  • - Increased the font size of the bus stop name, allowing more distinct hierarchy for the frame
  • - Removed the gray background of the bus lines and used lines instead to visually open up the design as well as making it easier to read
  • - The call to actions in the footer navigation bar were moved up since they were bleeding into the safe zone of the device
Final Stop

There were major changes from the second high fidelity iteration to the coded version because the client provided the asset containing the routes and arrival frequency of each bus that services the Washington and State bus stop at this stage.

Changes for the final iteration were:

  • - Removed "inbound/outbound" segmented control due to the multiple categories provided in the asset
  • - By listing all the bus lines and separating them by the categories, it is easier for the user to see all the transit options by just scrolling
  • - The icons in the header navigation bar in the paper and visual design prototype have been replaced with a menu icon. Since there could be additional calls to action that are important to users, such as accessing other favorites, checking on general alerts, or overal setting preferences. With multiple options, I compiled them into a menu, rather than have 4 or 5 icons in the frame.

Results and Takeaways


The final product solves the problem riders were having with the bus stop at Washington and State. BusyBus informs users the arrival times of buses servicing Washington and State. The product also provides users with a visual aid, mapping out the user's current location as well as the location of the desired bus stop.

A few takeaways from this project:

Expect the unexpected: The client's requirements can change at any point in time. I started off designing the app going in one direction, however when the client provided the content of the app, the design drastically changed to accomodate the information. The project taught me to be flexible and quick to adapt.

Lost in translation: BusyBus taught me to design realistically. Even if a designer creates the most aesthetically pleasing, user-friendly design, it may not translate well when coded. I learned to create designs that are not only user-friendly, but that also work well within the digital world.
