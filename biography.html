Allow me to introduce myself...

My earliest memory of expressing my artistic side was during my kindergarten ceramics class. I enjoyed taking something in its raw form and creating it into an art piece (by my 5 year-old standards and of course, my mom). I continued to mold and paint pottery until my mom claimed there was no more space at home to display my art work, even though I tried to make useful pieces, such as vases, bowls, etc. Although she didn't explicitly crush my dream to become a ceramist, I was always taught that art was just a hobby.

So I convinced myself I wasn't really a creative person anyways and focused my attention on different subjects.

Fast forward 20 years and I'm knee deep in Excel sheets filled with numbers and graphs. Life had guided me to the hospitality industry as a revenue analyst for hotels. Day in and day out, I analyzed the current market conditions and historical data to price hotels competitively.

I enjoyed the analysis part of the job, however, I was yearning for some type of creativity.

When creating excel reports, I found enjoyment in figuring out the best way to present data and the easiest way for users to find the data, whether it was adding a search tool or adding color to make something stand out. I contemplated a career change and a few friends who were doing UX design described to me their field and thought it would be suitable for me as I wanted to use both my right and left-brain. From what I heard about UX design, I'm able to analyze people and solve problems through design.

I felt I wanted to have a career that did more to help people and decided revenue management was not the way to fulfill that.

After a few years doing revenue management, I found the courage to do a career change towards UX design.

To start my career change process, I enrolled into the Thinkful design program. Through the program, I have discovered my passion that highlights my strengths. I always thought of myself as an empathetic person, trying to related to people and seeing something from a different point of view. I found that this characteristic is quite important for designing for a good user experience as well as branding. Although design is a big part of UX, solving problems and observing how people react to design decisions is the catalyst for the aesthetic portion.

I find that UX design is the perfect combination of tapping into my analytical side while expressing it through design.

When I'm not immersing myself in user personas and prototyping, I enjoy spending time in the kitchen, cooking and baking new recipes. I also like fitness, ranging from downward dog to burpees. Occasionally, the outdoor calls where I go hike the Dolomites or surf in Bali.

Thanks for stopping by!
